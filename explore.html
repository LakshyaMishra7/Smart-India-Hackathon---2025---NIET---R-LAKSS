Explore Sikkim's Monasteries

Discover peace, spirituality, and heritage in the Himalayas

Monasteries

Enchey Monastery

History

Built over 200 years ago, associated with Lama Drupthob Karpo, known for his flying powers.

Architecture

Traditional Tibetan style with ornate woodwork and images of protective deities.

Festivals

Hosts Chaam Dance Festival (Dec/Jan), celebrating victory of good over evil.

Visitor Info

3 km from Gangtok; best visited during festivals.

Rumtek Monastery

History

Seat of the Karmapa; originally built in the 16th century, rebuilt in the 1960s.

Architecture

Massive Tibetan-style structure with golden stupa and intricate murals.

Festivals

Main site for Kagyu rituals, with traditional Cham dances.

Visitor Info

24 km from Gangtok; entry fee applies for tourists.

Pemayangtse Monastery

History

Founded in the 17th century; one of the oldest monasteries of Sikkim.

Architecture

Known for its seven-tiered wooden structure "Zangdok Palri".

Festivals

Losar and Cham dance festivals draw many pilgrims.

Visitor Info

Located near Pelling; offers scenic views of Kanchenjunga.

Tashiding Monastery

History

Built in 17th century; believed to be blessed by Guru Padmasambhava.

Architecture

Famous for chortens and "Thongwa Rangdol" stupa.

Festivals

Hosts Bumchu festival in February/March.

Visitor Info

40 km from Yuksom; accessible by road.

Ralang Monastery

History

Built in 1995; important center of Kagyu tradition.

Architecture

Grand courtyard, giant prayer wheels, beautiful frescoes.

Festivals

Pang Lhabsol and Mahakala Dance celebrated here.

Visitor Info

6 km from Ravangla; panoramic views of the mountains.
